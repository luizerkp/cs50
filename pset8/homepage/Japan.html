Kon'nichiwa!!

Japan is an Island country that boast a rich history deeply rooted in tradition. It features beautiful mountains with the most famous being Mt. Fuji. It Also feature a host of unique islands like Rabbit Island, an Island populated by more rabbits than people, or Ao Island also known as Nekojima to the locals and "cat Island to foreigners. This island cat population outnumber humans by a ration of 6 to 1. When it comes to food, this goes without saying but you won't find better sushi than in its birth country. Food in Japan is experience that deeply recommend you get out of your comfort zone and experience as much of it as you can. From Ramen and seafood to steak and even more adventurous offerings like horse meat Sashimi, Japan has something great to offer to anyone not matter your comfort level. The great thing about japan is that it boasts one the most reliable and cleanest train systems in the world, this means that you can stay in Tokyo take a one day trip to see Mt Fuji and come back to Tokyo all by train. While visiting Japan unless you are in you of the smaller islands or in the more remote rural areas then you do not need Uber or taxi to move around efficiently. So don't be afraid go forth and explore.

Preparing to visit Japan

If you want to visit Japan, you first need to find out whether you can get a visa on arrival or you need to apply for one in advance. To find out more visit the The Ministry of Foreign affairs of Japan's website for the most up to date information on visa requirements and application process. I recommend ensuring your passport does not expire within 6 months and to apply for the visa, if needed, no less than 45 days before you depart. US citizens can get a visa on arrival that last for up to 90 days thus do not need to apply before when visiting for tourism purpose.

Planning the Trip:

The first thing you will want to do is to buy an airline ticket to Narita(NRT) or Haneda(HND); I have found Skyscanner to be very useful in getting the best deals on airfare, otherwise you can try the other big names like Expedia or Priceline. Which ever airport you use both have easy access to Tokyo and to transportation from one airport to the other. Therefore, if you find a great airfare deal but you arrive at HND and depart from NRT don't be afraid and take it. Before you leave the airport you will want to get a few things the first one is a mobile hotspot or sim navigating Japan will be a lot easier with internet access. The second thing you will want is a map of the railway system and take the time to plan out your route. It is highly recommended that you reserve the mobile hotspot/sim ahead of tike and to download the railway map before you arrive in Japan. The rail way map of Japan is huge, intimidating and can be a bit complicated but by the end of day one you will be an expert. From Tokyo you can take a train to most of the sightseeing destinations in Japan. On your first trip I recommend to take the shinaknsen (bullet train) to see Mt. Fuji for a day or two or if you fly into HND then you can take a coach bus. After return to Tokyo to explore the famous Tsukiji fish market to watch the tuna auctions at 5am, Akihabara for Anime lover, Sensoji Temple, Kawagoe to try on traditional Japanese clothing like a Yukata, DiverCity in Odaiba to see the giant Gundam and of course eat your way into poverty while exploring. As an optional route you can go shopping in Shinjuku, Shibuya or Ginza as well. Last but not least you should try out go karting in the streets of Tokyo at night although you can no longer dress as a Mario Kart real life character anymore after the Nintendo lawsuit. If you still want to test your go karting skills in Tokyo, please remember to obtain an international driving License before arriving in Japan or you won't be allowed to drive. You should plan to stay in Tokyo for 3 days minimum. If you have an extra day or two; I'd highly recommend visiting Nikko a small city located roughly a 2 hour train ride north of Tokyo. The city is filled with beautiful naturescapes and shrines. This will serve as way to get way from the hustle and bustle of Tokyo. Japan offers many more interesting locations not mentioned here, so go forth and explore.
