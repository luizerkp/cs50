  • While Thailand is not as harsh and draconian as let say N. Korea, there are somethings that are taboo in the country.
    Here are the top 5:

    1. Disrespecting the royal family in any way shape or form: While in Thailand you should keep all your comments about the royal family respectful. This is true out in the streets and online like social media.
    2. Climbing or touching Buddha statues, wearing or using the image of Buddha as decoration on clothing especially if that clothing will touch the floor in anyway... also tattoos depicting the image of Buddha are a big no no. Just be respectful of Buddha and you will be fine.
    3. Monks take an oath to touch women so if you are woman do not touch monks, even something as simple passing an offering or item to a Monk is not allowed. Also monks are highly respected in Thailand so treat them respectfully and respect their boundaries.
    4. Excessive PDAs(public displays of affection), while this won't get you in trouble per say it will get you odd looks. A quick peck in the cheek is not a problem but full on making out in public is frowned upon.
    5. Touching the head of someone older than you or passing something over their head is considered extremely rude.

  • When it comes to travel insurance, the decision is up to you; just like car insurance you buy it but hope to never have to use it. If you want a piece of mind you may purchase a travel insurance plane. They can cover everything from just you ticket to hospitalization or death. The plans are usually inexpensive can range from few 10USD to few 100USD. It depends on length of stay, age, pre-existing conditions, coverage ect. The decision is really up to you but if you are traveling within your home country then you don't need it. Traveling internationally it is highly recommended but not required especially if the credit card you used offers it and you have health insurance. Most health insurance plans cover you while you travel.

  • My rule of thumb is enough to cover incidentals plus some emergency cash (usually around 250USD-500USD). Ideally you want to carry as little cash on your as you can and make all purchase using credit. Avoid showing a lot of cash in public. Try to have a good credit card that won't gouge you on the exchange rate. Also try to pre-book all your excursions before you travel or online. If you don't have a good credit card then the goal is to to bring at least 50USD per person per day. You make use the hotel safe and only carry what you need fo that day. I you don't feel comfortable leaving stuff in the hotel safe then buy anti-theft bag and separate cash between different locations on your body only keeping a small amount in your pocket.

  • Avoid exchanging money at the airport you will never get the best rates that way. Also try to bring large denominations with you as those give out better rates. Avoid exchanging all you cash at once and only exchange as you need. Avoid exchanging or withdrawing money in secluded areas. Local exchange places can usually be reliably found in malls, movie theaters and in some hotels.
