Hola!!

The Dominican Republic is a wonderfully vibrant country, full of hearty people who love dancing and music which are a big part of the country's history and culture. From the beautiful beaches to it's long history, this small island has a lot to offer. The country features beautiful and luxurious all inclusive resorts in Puta Cana, one the main tourist destinations. It also offers rich history and art in the Zona Clonial, Santo Domingo. If you are looking for smaller crowds than in Punta Cana, you can try Playa Dorada, which translates to "golden beach" for its sand color. If feeling more adventurous why not take a trip to Saona Island, a beautiful small 42.47mi² island off Southeast coast of DR. The DR also offers some spectacular whale watching tours in Samaná Bay. You can also visit some impressive waterfalls of Damajagua in Puerto Plata or El Salto Limon Waterfall in Samaná. If the mountains are more your thing then take an adventure in Jarabacoa where it mountainous terrain make for some impressive hiking, biking and rafting. The DR is fast becoming one of the top tourist destinations of the Caribbean sea and for good reason; awesome food, party that never stops, drinks for the adults and awesome adventures for the kids all make this a place well worth visiting for the entire family.

Preparing to Visiting Dominican Republic:

If you want to visit The Dominican Republic you first need to find out whether you can get a visa on arrival or you need to apply for one in advance. To find out more visit the Dominican Consulate website for the most up to date information on visa requirements and application process. I recommend ensuring your passport does not expire within 6 months and to apply for the visa, if needed, no less than 45 days before you depart. The process takes about two weeks but you want to have some wiggle room in the event of any mishaps or delays. If are a citizen or have a visa to the US, EU, or UK you can get a visa on arrival and do not need to apply for one beforehand.

Planning the Trip:

The first thing you will want to do is to buy an airline ticket to Las Américas International Airport (SDQ) or to Punta Cana International Airport. Note that it can be cheaper to travel to SDQ and then take a transfer over to your final destination. For first time or inexperienced travelers; I recommend giving one for the all inclusive resorts a visit. They make it quite easy to either stay and enjoy the wonderful private breaches, the food and the drinks. Some resorts even come with their own mini-water parks, fun for the whole family. I have found Skyscanner to be very useful in getting the best deals on airfare, otherwise you can try the other big names like Expedia or Priceline. An alternative to big hotels are the private villas you can rent; these place a glamorous mansion style place where can stay in privacy and comfort. Some will even provide service staff like chefs and maids. The downside is these options tend to much more expensive than a traditional hotel or resort. Whichever route you go the Dominican Republic will make you want to come back to see more of this Island paradise.
