Hola!!

Mexico is a beautiful country with deep roots and a welcoming people. Home of the now extinct Mayan civilization known to have produced the world's most accurate calendar. You can visit one the new 7 wonders of the world Chichen-Itza, a Mayan ruin full of cultural significance and an impressive pyramid. When it comes to offerings Mexico really has it all whatever itches you adventurous bone, you can find it Mexico. Due in part to Hollywood/media and in part deserved; Mexico has a bad reputation for being a hot bed of violence due to drugs and the cartels; However the tourist areas are very safe and the cartels are isolated to certain cities. In other words, unless you go looking for trouble Mexico will be as safe as any other country you might visit so don't let those things stop you from visiting this wonderful country. Preparing to visit Mexico

If you want to visit Mexico you first need to find out whether you can get a visa on arrival or you need to apply for one in advance. To find out more visit the Mexican Embassy website for the most up to date information on visa requirements and application process. I recommend ensuring your passport does not expire within 6 months and to apply for the visa, if needed, no less than 45 days before you depart. The process usually takes about two weeks but you want to have some wiggle room in the event of any mishaps. If are a US citizen, permanent resident or have a visa to the US, UK, Canada or Japan; you can get a visa on arrival and thus do not need to apply beforehand.

Planning the Trip:

The first thing you will want to do is to buy an airline ticket to the airport closest to your desired destination, as Mexico is a big country we will focus on the Quintana Roo state of Mexico which includes Cancun, Playa del Carmen, Tulum and Cozumel as main tourist destinations. I have found Skyscanner to be very useful in getting the best deals on airfare, otherwise you can try the other big names like Expedia or Priceline. If it is your first time going to Mexico I highly recommend one of the aforementioned areas as your first destination. Whether you choose to stay in one area or hop around a bit, do not just spend your whole vacation cooped up in your hotel or resort. It will be tempting to just "chillax" by the pool sipping margaritas with maybe a water park or ziplining day thrown in the mix but you will miss out on some unique experiences like Cenotes which are large sinkholes or caves that are half submerged, think spelunking but in the water, or visiting one of the new 7 wonders of the world Chichen Itza or the beautiful and colorful city of Valladolid. If you are not claustrophobic you can try a Cenote tour, there are plenty of cenotes to choose from in the area and the hotel/ resort will likely have tour or a brochure to one, However I have found that the best ones are near or around the City of Tulum; if staying near or in Cancun the trip might be around 2.5 hrs but well worth it the day trip so plan accordingly. You can always just relax by the pool/beach/ or go ziplining/amusement park depending on your enrgy level. For amusement parks/ziplining we have Xel-Ha, Xplor, Ventura Park, Selvatica ect. Do note, that if staying at Xcaret resort depending on your reservation; you will have access to all their amusement parks and tours which also includes a cenote tour and a Chichen Itza tour. Cozumel is also a choice if you want do is relax, zip on a beverage of choice and admire beautiful white sand beaches or view some beautiful undeveloped and undisturbed naturescapes. Mexico has plenty of options not matter how adventurous you are, it has delicious food and wondaful people so don't be afraid give Mexico a try you won't be disappointed.
